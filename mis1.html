3. Concept of MIS

4. Definition of MIS:

MIS is popularly known as the Information System the Information and Decision System and the computer based Information System.

The MIS has more than one definition , some of which are given below:

  • MIS is defined as a system which provides information support for decision - making in the organization.
  • MIS is defined as an integrated the information to support the operations, the management and the decision-making function in the organization.
  • MIS is defined as computer - based Information system.

All the above definition converge on one single point i.e. the MIS is a computerized business processing system generating information for the people in the organization to meet the information needs for decision-making to achieve the objectives of the organization.

To understand the management information systems, it is possible to define it into three consistent

  • Management:
    Defined as process, a function, and a profession dealing with the activity of getting work done with and through people. The functions of management include planning, organizing, directing, controlling, staffing, leading, and motivating.
  • Informtion:
    Could be defined as a set of facts, figures, and symbols processed foor the curret decision-making situation. It's considered to be of significance in a particular situation.
  • System:
    Defined as a set of related components activities, processes, and human beings interacting together to accomplish some common objective.

Role of the MIS

The role of the MIS in an organization can be compared to the role of the heart in the human body. Information is the blood, and heart in the MIS system collects, processes, and distributes, appropriate data to all parts of the organization, ensuring the information needs of every individual, group, and manager are met.

Key roles of MIS

  • Supports for clerical and Junior Management:
    The MIS helps clerical personal with transaction proceesing and answers their queries on data, records, and documents. It also provides jnior management with operational data for planning, Scheduling, and control, and hepls them make decisions to correct out - of - control situations.
  • Support for Middle Management: It assists middle management with shoet - term planning, and evoiving business plans and their implementation.
  • General Functions: The MIS plays a vital role in information geneartion, communication, and problem identification, all of which are essential for the decision - making of which are essential for the decision - making process. It also plays a vital role in the overall management, administration, and operations of an organization.

14 Principles of Henri Fayol

  1. Division of Work:
    This results in the functional division of an organization into departments.
  2. Authority:
    Authority is required to give orders to subordinates
  3. Discipline:
    There should be clear, understand and fair agreements as to when discipline may be exercised upon the subordinates.
  4. Unity of Command:
    Under which subordinate should receive orders from one superior only
  5. Unity of direction:
    For any one activity, with one objective only, one plan and one leader.
  6. Unity of direction:
    For any one activity, with one objective only, one plan and leader.
  7. Subordination of individual interest:
    Interests of one individual or group shall not prevail over the whole.
  8. Remuneration:
    The reemuneration of the personnel should be fair to both the employee and the organization.
  9. Degree of Centralization:
    In which the responsiblity of decision making, policy formulation and resource commitment are concentrated in the hands of senior management in the upper levels of organization hierarchy.
  10. Scalar chain:
    Scalar chain is drawn to represent a continuos line of authority from the top to the bottom of the hierarchy.
  11. Order:
    Social order ensures the fluid opeartion of a company through authoritative procedure. Material order ensures safety and efficiency in the workplace.
  12. Equity:
    Employees must be treated kindly, and justice must be enacted to ensure a just workplace.
  13. Stability of tenuree of Personnel:
    The period of service should not be too short and employyes should not be moved from positions frequently.
